## Assignments

### 1. AXON Networks (`axon-networks/`)

Deep research into AXON Networks, a telecom AI company, covering:

- **Product Architecture** — NEURA platform, digital twin infrastructure, and Operations-as-a-Service (OaaS) model
- **Multi-Agent System Design** — How AXON's domain-specific agent approach compares to generic frameworks like LangChain and AutoGen
- **Geopolitical Analysis** — Implications of the AXON–Cassava Technologies partnership for pan-African telecom infrastructure, AI sovereignty, and emerging market dynamics
- **Cybersecurity** — Vulnerabilities in closed-loop autonomous AI systems operating on live broadband infrastructure (agent hijacking, digital twin breach, multi-tenant attack surfaces)
- **Build vs Buy** — Architectural tradeoffs between proprietary agent frameworks and open-source alternatives

---

### 2. AI Agents — Taxonomy and Ontologies (`AI AGENTS-taxonomy and ontologies/`)

Structured research into the classification and formal modeling of multi-agent AI systems, covering:

- **Framework Analysis** — LangChain, LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen from first principles, including failure modes and deployment-focused variants (OpenClaw, NemoClaw, ZeroClaw)
- **Multi-Dimensional Taxonomy** — Classifying agentic systems by coordination style, autonomy level (L1–L5), and deployment context (digital vs physical/embodied AI)
- **Ontology Mapping** — Complete relationship model: agent ↔ memory, tools, environment, other agents, audit layer, and governance policy
- **Physical AI Gap** — How taxonomy and ontology frameworks must evolve for embodied AI, sensor noise, and real-world consequence severity
- **Cybersecurity Dimension** — Prompt injection, agent hijacking, inter-agent communication attacks, and audit trail manipulation
